Rick and Morty: The Fight Between Rick and the Rest

Rick and Morty is a TV cartoon show with 4 seasons consisting of 41 total episodes. The idea of the show is travelling with a genius grandpa (Rick) and his grandson (Morty) into parallel universes where they encounter endless unique and creative locations and entities. Those two along with the rest of the family have constant quarrels and fights with sarcastic delivery which makes it a hoot to watch and really easy to binge. If you would like to see more on the overall summary of the show, here is a link to their Wikipedia page

Of the relationships which I enjoy to watch, it is incredibly fun to see how Rick disapproves of his son-in-law, Jerry, and all the doofy things he does. That along with the encounters between Rick and his daughter Beth because of how sarcastic the conversations are between those two as well. Rick is definetly the person who 'stirs the pot' and creates the most issues, but at the same time, he fixes the most stuff and cares a crazy amount about his family.

The reason why Rick disapproves is because of Jerry is because Jerry is incredibly insecure and gets himself into situations where he is obviously seen as the 'beta', all the while Rick being an extreme 'alpha'. Ultimately, each one of the times Rick and Jerry have an encounter, Rick is put into a position where he must save Jerry because he cannot manage himself in the situations he gets into. For the most part, Jerry is not in many episodes, and when he is, there is a lot of back and forth between him and the rest of his family. It pretty much seems as though the only role Jerry really needs to play is the person who everyone makes fun of.
